# Public Claims Ledger

This ledger controls the claims allowed on the public website. Public language may be commercially clear, but it must stay precise, bounded, and reviewable.

## Buyer-value wording guardrail

Public wording may describe pain, risk, value, review outcomes, and controlled walkthroughs. It may explain why earlier visibility, evidence preservation, scoped AI action control, missing-evidence review, and materiality-ranked engineering findings matter to buyers.

Public wording must not describe exact implementation recipes, country source maps, scoring formulas, proof schemas, internal architecture, private dashboards, product code, production readiness, customer traction, compliance outcomes, legal determinations, or autonomous decisions.

## Disclosure Tiers

| Tier | Public treatment |
| --- | --- |
| Public principle | Explain one exact action, clearance bound into a receipt, protected-target verification before change, bounded rejection behavior, reviewable evidence, and TG360 inspection. |
| Private walkthrough | Demonstrate richer action-clearance context, accepted/rejected synthetic scenarios, evidence lineage, protected-target outcomes, DORA-oriented mapping, data-protection accountability, architecture roles, and claims boundaries. |
| NDA / design-partner private | Keep private contracts, receipt schema, provenance and reuse mechanics, target synchronization, adapters, threat model, topology, credentials, and source code out of the public surface. |
